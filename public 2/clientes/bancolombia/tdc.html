What is it?

A data platform that consolidates and centralizes all transactional and operational information for payment method products, including:

  • Prepaid Cards
  • Digital Payments (wallets, tokenization, subscriptions)

What for?

To facilitate various processes and ensure a single source of truth:

What it is NOT

It is not a replacement for the core. It is an intermediate layer for consumption, consolidation, and cross-cutting data availability.

2. Fundamental Architectural Principles

üì¶

Separation and Independence by Product

Avoid technical or organizational couplings that hinder the bank's future evolution.

  • Each product will have its own "data sheet," processed independently.
  • Changes in a core (e.g., VisionPlus) do not require redesign of the rest of the system.
  • Facilitates product mobility between organizational units without technical impact.

"I've always said: we have to keep their product technology independent." ‚Äî Didier

Replication, Transformation, and Enrichment

Data is brought from core systems and goes through a transformation process.

  • A solution already exists to replicate data from cores (e.g., VisionPlus).
  • Product "sheets" are transformed for specific uses (statements, collections, debt).
  • Flexibility in transformation technologies (buckets, relational databases, NoSQL) is contemplated.

3. Building and Consuming the Central Repository

üìä

The Centralized Repository

The central repository does not yet exist but is being designed. MongoDB is a strong candidate compared to Aurora PostgreSQL.

  • Consolidate different data sheets into a single point of analysis and exposure.
  • Allow cross-cutting queries (by client, card type, date).
  • Support horizontal elasticity and modular growth.

"MongoDB makes our lives much easier." ‚Äî Frank

üîå

Multi-channel Consumption of the Repository

Data will be consumed by various internal bank systems and teams:

  • UCS: Internal operational support platform
  • Physical Terminals: For bank users or employees
  • Power BI: Business dashboards and reporting
  • RPA: Internal automations
  • APIs: Interfaces to expose information to internal systems

  • Legal or operational reporting and audits.

Integration and Evolution Strategy

Key considerations to ensure adaptability and growth:

  • Transformation layer already in design (events, ETLs, APIs for updates).

MongoDB vs Aurora PostgreSQL

Bancolombia has already evaluated Aurora and sees it as rigid. They expect MongoDB to demonstrate:

  • Technical Value: For modular, multi-product architecture.
  • Flexibility: In evolution, independence, and cross-cutting consumption.
  • Cost-Benefit: A competitive proposal.
