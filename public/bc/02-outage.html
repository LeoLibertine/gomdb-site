What happened

When the core went down, the bank went dark.

During a scheduled maintenance window, an error on a critical machine in the legacy core cascaded. Because nearly every customer-facing service reads and writes straight through that core, the failure didn't stay contained — it became a domino. App, web, branches, ATMs: degraded or down for days.

The realization

The customer should never lose the ability to ask.

Here's the lesson the bank took away — and the heart of Monday's conversation. Most of what a customer does isn't moving money; it's asking about it: "what's my balance?", "did my transfer land?", "show my last movements." Those questions are the lifeline of trust.

From discovery— confirmed by the bank's technical lead
~70% askingnon-monetary: balances, statements, movement queries
~30% movingmonetary: transfers, payments
Roughly 7 of every 10 hits on the deposits core are pure asking — and every one of them lands on the same expensive heart that has to hold the moving-money safe.

So the bank is looking for a way to keep that asking alive and fast — independent of whether the heart is healthy. Separate the asking from the moving-money, so a hiccup in the core never again means a customer staring at a blank screen. That's the door you'll walk through Monday.
