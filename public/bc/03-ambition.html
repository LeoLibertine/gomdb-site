The pressure

The neobanks aren't coming. They're here.

In the bank's own words, Revolut arrived with a "super aggressive" proposition — and today they have no digital product that comes close. Nubank is already peeling off customers. The incumbent's market dominance still cushions the blow, but the projection is clear: intermediation margins will shrink over the next few years. The mandate from leadership is blunt — be more efficient, and compete.

And the customer's yardstick is no longer "another bank." It's Uber, Amazon, Mercado Libre. People expect their bank to feel instant, personal, and intelligent. Lagging that expectation, on a 30-year-old core, is an existential problem dressed up as a UX problem.

The leap they want

Three doors the bank wants to open.

These are the experiences Banco Continental wants to ship — the kind neobanks already offer, and the kind a legacy core makes painfully hard.

Conversational AI

A financial assistant customers can simply talk to in plain language to understand their money — clear, human answers instead of menus and cryptic codes.

Real-time fraud

Catch suspicious patterns in milliseconds, not hours — protecting the most vulnerable customers without adding friction for everyone else.

Alternative scoring

Turn everyday signals — payments, subscriptions, behavior — into credit opportunity for the millions the traditional system simply doesn't "see."

Where this is heading

AI is plugging straight into the bank.

This isn't a someday idea. In May 2026, OpenAI launched a personal-finance experience inside ChatGPT in the US — customers link their bank accounts and ask an assistant about their own money. The strategic warning for incumbents was blunt: if a general AI owns the intelligent conversation, the bank risks being reduced to back-end plumbing.

The industry signal

Open banking and AI agents push the same way — the intelligence flows to whoever holds clean, connected financial data. So the question for Banco Continental isn't whether these experiences arrive. It's whether the bank owns them, or rents them out.

Either path leads to the same prerequisite: customer data that's clean, unified, real-time and AI-ready. That's the unlock a modern operational data layer provides — what turns these use cases from fragile experiments into products the bank can actually ship.
