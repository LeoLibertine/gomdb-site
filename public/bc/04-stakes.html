The pain points

Where it hurts today.

These are the explicit pains the bank is living with — the reasons this conversation exists.

Single point of failure

One core outage takes down every channel at once — app, web, branches, ATMs.

Costly mainframe reads

Every balance check and query burns expensive MIPS — and roughly 7 of every 10 hits are exactly this kind of non-monetary asking.

Losing the digital race

No product that matches Revolut or Nubank — the bank is conceding digital ground.

Frozen innovation

Modern and AI experiences simply can't ship on a rigid, 30-year-old core.

Regulatory & reputational exposure

Outages bring scrutiny, penalties, and eroded trust the bank can't afford.

Data locked & slow

Different teams can't query operational data in real time — every insight needs a slow data-warehouse detour.

The business objectives

What they want to achieve.

And here — explicitly — are the business outcomes the bank is trying to move. These are the goals you're helping them reach.

Increase revenue

Launch new AI-enabled products and services that open fresh revenue streams.

Reduce cost

Take reads off the mainframe (fewer MIPS) and use cloud elasticity to cut cost-to-income.

Cut outage cost & risk

Avoid the next multi-day, multi-million disruption — and the remediation bill behind it.

Meet regulation

Auditable data and faster regulatory reporting; reduce exposure to penalties and claims.

Improve the customer experience

Instant, personal, neobank-grade UX that lifts NPS and customer loyalty.

A secure data layer

Encryption, data masking, Zero Trust and full audit — security built into the model.

Always-on resilience

Serve customers through outages — continuity as the default, not an emergency response.

Compete & grow

Capture deposits and defend market share against aggressive new entrants.

Reduce time-to-market

Launch new products and features faster — without months of legacy schema changes for every iteration.

Real-time analytics across the business

One source of truth — risk, finance, regulatory and product teams all querying the same operational data in real time, instead of waiting on last night's batch.
