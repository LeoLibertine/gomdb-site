Current Challenges

Critical Pain Points

!

Scalability Issues

Current architecture doesn't allow for easy scalability or functional growth. The platform operates on a monolithic architecture ('big block') with high complexity that creates significant risks with every change implemented. The system was not designed to handle future transaction volumes or complexity.

!

Performance Bottlenecks

Fund dispersal calculations take approximately 3 hours using previous day's data. Transactional tables with over 200 million rows cause severe latency in computing account statuses and wallet balances. The transactional model involves up to 5 updates per transaction with complex joins, creating cascading performance issues.

!

Operational Risk

The platform is critical in Mexico with zero tolerance for downtime (RPO and RTO of zero). If a failure occurs during fund dispersal, reprocessing could cause operational breaches as dispersions must be completed by noon. This directly affects customer experience with risks of customer churn, reputational damage, and negative cash flow impact.

!

Technical Debt

The codebase is a mix of Java, PHP, Python, and Node.js with no microservices foundation. Running on AWS EC2 with Tomcat, Docker containers, and 18 MySQL replicas. Parts of the system are over 10 years old. The frontend presents security vulnerabilities and stability issues. Database and business logic associated with reconciliation processes are particularly problematic.

!

Reporting & Analytics Delays

Report generation takes excessively long, impacting departments from commercial to finops, finance, and fund distribution. Engineering and infrastructure teams are experiencing serious difficulties maintaining and optimizing the current system.

Proposed Migration Strategy

Phase 1: Assessment & Mapping

Complete functional mapping of current system to identify critical components, business flows, and key dependencies. Map all 170+ stored procedures, understand integration points with Prosa and other systems. Document all reconciliation processes and business logic. Identify quick wins for immediate impact.

Phase 2: Parallel Architecture

Build new microservices-based architecture supported by serverless services like AWS Lambda. Create distributed structure facilitating maintenance, control, and sustainable growth. Design clean, modular, scalable, and autonomous components. Leverage existing connections to accelerate and reduce risk while avoiding dependencies on legacy systems.

Phase 3: Gradual Migration

Gradually redirect traffic to new platform with controlled testing in real environments without disrupting operations. Implement processes ensuring traceability, version control, transactional monitoring, and reversibility. Include automated testing, parallel QA environments, and rollback plans per service. Deploy each component with prior validation.

Phase 4: Full Cutover

Complete migration with zero transactional loss, ensuring RPO/RTO of zero. Temporarily redirect traffic to new platform with ability to revert to current system if needed, reconciling data to continue operations without impact. Critical for Mexico operations where no downtime is acceptable. Final decommissioning of legacy MySQL infrastructure.

Estimated timeline: 6 months traditional approach / 2-3 months for Phase 1 with MongoDB Factory

Note: Customer estimates full modernization at 6 months traditionally, but is open to running a scoped first phase in 2-3 months if MongoDB's Factory team demonstrates accelerated delivery path.
